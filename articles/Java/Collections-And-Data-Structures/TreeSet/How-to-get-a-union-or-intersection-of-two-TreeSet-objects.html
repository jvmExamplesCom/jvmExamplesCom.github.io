How to get a union or intersection of two TreeSet objects

Both TreeSet in Java and HashSet offer methods for obtaining the union and intersection of two sets, however due to implementation differences there are slight variations of these methods for each implementation. In this article, we will discuss how to get the union and intersection of two TreeSet objects in Java.

Syntax for Union and Intersection of Two TreeSet Objects

The syntax to obtain the union and intersection of two TreeSet objects is as follows: 

          
           
TreeSet<String> firstSet = new TreeSet<String>();
TreeSet<String> secondSet = new TreeSet<String>();

// Generate the union.
firstSet.addAll(secondSet);

// Generate the intersection.
firstSet.retainAll(secondSet);

Example 1: Union of Two TreeSet Objects

Let’s take a look at a simple example of obtaining the union of two TreeSet objects. In the following code, we create two TreeSet objects and populate each with a few elements. We then obtain the union of these two sets by calling the 

          
           
addAll()
method. 

          
           
TreeSet<String> firstSet = new TreeSet<String>();
firstSet.add("Apple");
firstSet.add("Banana");
firstSet.add("Orange");

TreeSet<String> secondSet = new TreeSet<String>();
secondSet.add("Kiwi");
secondSet.add("Mango");
secondSet.add("Banana");

// Generate the union.
firstSet.addAll(secondSet);

// Print the union.
System.out.println("The union of the two sets is: " + firstSet);
In the above example, we get the following output when we print the union of the two sets: 

          
           
The union of the two sets is: [Apple, Banana, Kiwi, Mango, Orange]

Now let’s take a look at an example of obtaining the intersection of two TreeSet objects. In the following code, we create two TreeSet objects and populate each with a few elements. We then obtain the intersection of these two sets by calling the 

          
           
retainAll()

TreeSet<String> firstSet = new TreeSet<String>();
