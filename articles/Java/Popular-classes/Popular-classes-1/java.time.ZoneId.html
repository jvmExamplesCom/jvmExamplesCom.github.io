Before delving into the specifics of Java.time.ZoneId, it is important to understand some basics of time zones. Time zones are regions of the world where the same standard time is used. This is required as the earth rotates from west to east meaning that different parts of the earth experience different times in a day. For example, when it is 3 pm in New York, it is 6 pm in London. To keep track of these different times, different parts of the world use different standards of time which are based on their geographical coordinates.
